# Quick Recipes

## Masala Chai
Masala Chai is a spiced Indian milk tea. To make 2 cups: boil 1 cup of water with 1 inch of crushed ginger, 2 cardamom pods, 1 small cinnamon stick, and 3 cloves for 3 minutes. Add 1.5 teaspoons of strong black tea (CTC preferred) and boil for 1 more minute. Add 1 cup of whole milk and simmer until it starts to foam. Sweeten with 2 teaspoons of sugar. Strain and serve hot.

## Avocado Toast
Toast 2 slices of sourdough bread until golden. While toasting, mash 1 ripe avocado with the juice of half a lemon, a pinch of salt, and chili flakes. Spread the mash generously on the toasted bread. Top with a poached egg, cherry tomatoes, and microgreens. Drizzle with olive oil and serve immediately. Optional: add feta cheese or smoked salmon.

## Overnight Oats
Combine ½ cup of rolled oats with ½ cup of milk (dairy or plant-based) and ¼ cup of plain yogurt in a jar. Add 1 tablespoon of chia seeds and 1 teaspoon of honey or maple syrup. Stir well, seal the jar, and refrigerate overnight (at least 6 hours). In the morning, top with fresh berries, sliced banana, and a spoonful of nut butter. No cooking required.

## Dal Tadka
Pressure cook 1 cup of yellow lentils (moong or toor dal) with 2 cups of water, salt, and turmeric for 3 whistles. In a pan, heat 2 tablespoons of ghee, add 1 teaspoon of cumin seeds, 2 dried red chilies, and 4 garlic cloves. Fry until golden, then add 1 chopped onion and 2 tomatoes. Cook until soft. Pour the dal into the pan, mix well, and simmer for 5 minutes. Garnish with fresh coriander and a squeeze of lemon.
